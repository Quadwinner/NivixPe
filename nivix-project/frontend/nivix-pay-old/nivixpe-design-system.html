01 — Primary Color Strategy

Two Families.
One Coherent Signal.

NivixPe operates at the intersection of finance and blockchain. The color system must communicate two things simultaneously: the trustworthiness of a bank and the innovation of a crypto-native platform. The strategy is to anchor on Navy Blue as the trust signal, then layer Ocean Teal as the innovation and blockchain signal — with a deep connector ramp bridging them into a coherent premium identity.

Primary
Navy Blue
Anchor colour for CTAs, buttons, links, and primary interactive states. Communicates trust, financial stability, and institutional credibility.
#0A4174
Psychology: Trust · Authority · Stability
Secondary
Ocean Teal
Innovation colour for blockchain moments, on-chain badges, Solana features, and secondary actions. Signals speed, transparency, and the future of finance.
#0C7075
Psychology: Innovation · Flow · Transparency
Connector
Deep Blue
Bridge colour for gradients, dark surfaces, and mid-level emphasis. Unifies Navy and Teal into a seamless depth hierarchy for backgrounds and cards.
#294D61
Psychology: Depth · Precision · Calmness

Why this spectrum works for fintech

Blue is the universal trust colour in financial services — JPMorgan, Visa, PayPal, and virtually every successful payment brand uses it as their anchor. It reduces anxiety in high-stakes transactions and signals reliability. Teal adds the differentiation fintech needs: it signals technology, freshness, and innovation without the recklessness of red or the coldness of pure grey. Together they make NivixPe feel simultaneously as safe as a bank and as fast as Solana.

The Deep Blue connector ramp is what makes the system feel premium rather than just functional. It gives depth to dark backgrounds, creates rich card layering, and ensures the hero gradients feel like a single brand moment rather than two colors in a room.

03 — Typography System

Three Typefaces.
Zero Ambiguity.

Every surface in NivixPe uses one of three fonts. Sora for everything that represents the brand voice — headlines, CTAs, labels. DM Sans for all readable body content. Space Mono for all financial data, transaction hashes, and amounts. This three-way split eliminates font choice decisions and creates an immediately recognisable typographic fingerprint.

08 — Do's, Don'ts & Final Palette

Rules of Engagement.

DO
✓
Use Navy-600 (#0A4174) for all primary CTAs, buttons, and interactive elements. This is the trust anchor — consistency builds user confidence in financial transactions.
✓
Reserve Teal-500 (#0C7075) for on-chain confirmations, Solana-specific features, and blockchain moments. Scarcity maintains its signal value.
✓
Always use Space Mono for every number, amount, address, hash, and rate displayed to the user. Never mix with DM Sans in the same number.
✓
Use semantic tokens (e.g. --cta-bg) in all component code — never raw hex values. One place to update = changes everywhere.
✓
Let dark backgrounds step gradually — Ink-950 for page, Ink-900 for cards, Ink-800 for elevated elements. Depth creates premium feel.
✓
Pair color with icon + text for all tx states — never rely on color alone. A colorblind user must still understand if a transaction succeeded or failed.
✓
Use the brand gradient for hero sections, landing pages, and dashboard headers only. Not for buttons, badges, or inline UI elements.
DON'T
✗
Don't use Teal as the primary CTA. It is the secondary/blockchain colour — using it for primary actions creates confusion about what on-chain means.
✗
Don't hardcode hex values in component code. Every color reference must go through the token layer — no exceptions, even for one-off fixes.
✗
Don't mix DM Sans with Space Mono in the same number or amount string. Pick one. Financial data must always use Space Mono exclusively.
✗
Don't apply the brand gradient to buttons. Gradient CTAs are difficult to manage state variants for (hover, focus, disabled) and feel cheap in fintech contexts.
✗
Don't use Error (#FF4D4F) or Warning (#FFB800) decoratively. Semantic overload destroys user trust — if everything is red, nothing is urgent.
✗
Don't introduce off-system colours without a formal token addition. One rogue purple in a fintech dashboard signals unprofessionalism to investors and users.
✗
Don't use pure white (#FFFFFF) as a page background in light mode — use Ink-50 (#F4F6F9). Pure white creates eye strain in financial dashboards with dense data.
